Issue No. 02 • Week of June 26, 2026

Feeder Space Tightens as Peak Season Peaks Early

The global container market is in active peak-season mode, and that pressure is reaching our region. Transpacific rates jumped again this week as carriers held June GRIs and Peak Season Surcharges firm. As mainline capacity is pulled toward those high-rate east–west lanes, secondary trades — including the Gulf of America and Caribbean Basin — are tightening. Below is our read for the week, plus a route focus on Venezuela.

1 · Global Ocean Freight Snapshot

Macro drivers: Bunker costs have eased — down ~12% since early June and ~25% from March highs — but remain roughly 40% above February levels. A US–Iran memorandum is gradually reopening the Strait of Hormuz, beginning with feeder services. Carriers have announced steep further increases for July, with some targeting Asia–Europe rates up to $3,000/FEU higher. The early peak means meaningful relief is unlikely before Q4.

U.S. Sanctions & Compliance — Venezuela

Shipping from the U.S. to Venezuela is subject to active U.S. Treasury OFAC sanctions. While cargo is not categorically prohibited, it requires strict compliance: full SDN screening of all parties, no shipments to government-owned/controlled entities without a specific OFAC license, restricted USD payment channels, and complete booking details at time of request. Stella Line handles this discipline as part of every Venezuela booking, but recommends all customers obtain independent legal and sanctions-compliance counsel. Stella Line does not provide legal or sanctions advice.

4 · Outlook & Commercial Recommendations
  • Book early — peak-season pressure is real. Capacity is tight and rates are rising on multiple lanes. Secure July/August space and rates now; waiting for spot rates to drop is a high-risk strategy.
  • Lock in rates where possible. For recurring Caribbean cargo, consider a short-term (30–90 day) contract rate. Spot rates are volatile and likely to keep rising through Q3 2026.
  • Budget conservatively for fuel. A gradual Strait of Hormuz reopening may ease bunker costs eventually, but not soon — do not assume surcharges drop in Q3.
  • Venezuela — prepare compliance first. OFAC screening and carrier pre-approval must be completed before any booking. Allow 3–5 business days lead time for compliance processing.
  • Project & break-bulk windows. For oversized or heavy-lift cargo, June–early July may still offer reasonable rates on feeder trades before peak fully hits. Contact us to discuss vessel options.
