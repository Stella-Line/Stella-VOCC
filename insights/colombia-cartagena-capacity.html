Cartagena capacity: what the transshipment squeeze means for Colombia cargo

July 9, 2026 Stella Line — Market Desk 2 min read

Cartagena is one of the Caribbean's busiest transshipment hubs — which is exactly why Colombia cargo gets caught in swings that have nothing to do with Colombia itself. When boxes stack up waiting for connections, both Cartagena and Barranquilla feel it, and rates from the U.S. Gulf and South Florida move accordingly.

With longer dwell times possible on U.S. Gulf → Cartagena connections, the booking math shifts for July and August cargo: the window for reasonable break-bulk pricing narrows, and it pays to lock space early rather than chase spot. For Barranquilla specifically, river-port constraints make berth timing the variable to watch. Talk to us before you commit — small routing changes can save real money on this lane right now.
